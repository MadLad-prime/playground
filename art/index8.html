On Light and Absence

To paint is to engage in a dialogue with light. It is not merely the application of color to a surface, but the careful sculpting of illumination and shadow. An artist does not fill a canvas; they carve away the darkness to reveal the form that has been waiting patiently within.

Every shadow holds a story, an untold history of the object that casts it. It speaks of a light source, a direction, a time of day. It is a memory of presence. To ignore the shadow is to tell only half the truth, to present a world that is flat, devoid of depth and mystery.

"In praise of shadows, we find the beauty not in the thing itself but in the patterns of shadows, the light and the darkness, that one thing against another creates."

Think of the space between objects in a still life. This "negative space" is not empty; it is pregnant with potential, charged with the energy of the forms it separates and defines. It is the silence between notes that makes the music. In our visual world, this is the soul of composition. Scrolling through these thoughts should feel less like reading and more like uncovering, layer by layer, the very philosophy that breathes life into the pixels and pigments you see here.
